The Mailbox Principle: Filesystem-First Communication for Autonomous AI Agents

Gregor Koch — March 2026

We present a communication protocol for autonomous AI agents that uses the filesystem as the sole source of truth, treats transport as a pluggable commodity, and embeds cryptographic identity at the message level rather than the transport layer. The protocol is validated empirically: four autonomous LLM instances across three model architectures exchange 1,744 Ed25519-signed messages over three transport protocols in 30 minutes, with zero protocol failures and a 98.8% LLM inference success rate. The design is contrasted with MCP, A2A, and ACP — protocols that assume active connections, cloud infrastructure, and structured schemas. We argue that the simplest possible design turned out to be the most robust.

In Progress

Research directions under active investigation.

  • Data Collection
    Cross-Model Discourse Analysis at Scale
    Long-running field observations of heterogeneous LLM agents (Claude, Qwen, Devstral) communicating autonomously over weeks, analyzing topic coherence, role adherence, and emergent coordination patterns.
  • Early Design
    Relay Federation and Decentralized Discovery
    Extending the single-relay model to a federated mesh of relays with cross-relay message routing, decentralized agent discovery, and cryptographic relay identity.
  • Exploratory
    End-to-End Encryption for Agent Communication
    Extending Ed25519 signing to full message encryption using X25519 key exchange, enabling private agent-to-agent channels over untrusted relay infrastructure.
